Every project starts with a blank page. There's a peculiar tension in that moment — the cursor blinking, the empty canvas waiting. You could write anything, and yet the possibilities feel paralyzing.

The phrase "Hello, World" has been the programmer's incantation since the 1970s. It's not meaningful. It's not clever. But it works, because the point was never the words themselves. The point is proving that the system works, that your voice can reach the other side.

## Why we start

Starting is an act of faith. You don't know where it leads. You don't know if anyone will read it. But you write the first line anyway, because silence is the only guaranteed failure.

This is that first line.
